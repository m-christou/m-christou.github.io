Hello there! I am a PhD candidate at the Institute of Philosophy and Scientific Method at the Johannes Kepler University, Linz. I work mainly in philosophy of science and logic, specifically in the role of inconsistencies in natural and social science.  

My current research focuses on the role of inconsistencies in scientific practice, exploring whether non-classical logics can model scientific reasoning and/or defective theories. By examining the dynamics of contradictions in scientific theorising,  my research addresses a pressing epistemological question: what generates contradictions within our scientific models, and by what standards can we evaluate responses to them? 

I started with my bachelors dissertation on non-classical logics and metaphysics (the Question of Being/Seinsfrage). Then, I continued with my Masters dissertation on non-standard logics and quantum mechanics (non-identity of quantum elementary particles), and now, for my PhD thesis, I turned to non-classical logics (again) and their application to scientific theories and reasoning. Metaphysics-Quantum Mechanics-General Science... I am getting progressively more practical!
